Since the day his father taught him how to run ‘Prince of Persia’ on MS-DOS, Berk Ozzambak has always been interested in computers in technology. Initially having a social sciences degree (BA on International Relations), his first job in a start-up made him realize the power of the software development and its current and potential impact on every possible subject.

From then on, he started practicing programming, mainly by following online classes from Harvard University (Certificate) and FreeCodeCamp (Certificate) until his application and acceptence to the Software Engineering degree on CODE University of Applied Sciences, Berlin, Germany. At present, he is studying his second academic year meanwhile undertaking the Teaching Assistant position for the Software Engineering Faculty.

While being keen to explore the different branches of software development, he is currently focusing on web development technologies, practicing front and backend frameworks of the JavaScript predominantly (Vue.js / Node.js & Express) His ongoing and completed projects can be viewed in the below section.

In addition to technology, Berk is a music lover, a semi-professional guitar player, father of two dogs and a passionate basketball fan. He is a native Turkish speaker, proficient in English (IELTS 8), following classes in intermediate level German (B1) and has beginner level skills in Chinese.
